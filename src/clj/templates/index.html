Birdwave Visualizing bird migrations

How it works

The choropleth above displays the movement of various birds across the United States over a period of a year.

The data for this example was obtained from eBird.org. It consists of all the sightings that were reported for the entire US region over the period of an year from December 2012 through November 2013, and weighs in at almost 30M records (20 GB) of tab-separated values.

The example retrieves the per-county sighting data for a given bird taxon and month, and calculates the ratio between the number of sightings in each county and the total number of birds that were sighted across those sightings. It then selectively changes the color of each county to reflect that ratio.

To see the movement of any of the listed birds, click on a bird in the list to select it. Then drag the slider month to month, and see how the bird population changes over time. You can also zoom in/out by clicking on a state.
